    IMDb Movie Dashboard


    The data came from a U.S. government dataset. This project aims to analyze the top five rated movies in the world based on the revenue they generated.

    In the first graph, the number of titles is broken down by genre in a clustered column chart. Its purpose is to analyze the top five genres of movies based on release titles. In the second chart, the titles of movies are analyzed based on their genres and ratings. The chart shows the ratings of titles in a bare roll-up of genres and the rating segments for each film. The last chart shows the trend in total revenue by year using an area chart. Analyzing the revenue generated by each film from 1995 to 2015 was done using this chart.

    Eventually, the rating was added to see if more interesting rating trends have emerged and if movie types are generating more or less revenue over time. Over time, this stacked area chart shows how the distribution has changed along with the value. According to the analysis, comedy movies rated PG and G had the most viewers from 2000 through 2010, while G movies had the highest revenue of $86,518,059 and PG movies had $70,002,097.
